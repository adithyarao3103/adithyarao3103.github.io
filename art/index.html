Art Gallery

Indian architecture is a treasure trove for art lovers. Each carving on the walls of the temples is a masterpiece and a treat to your eyes. I love putting a perspective to these carvings by reproducing them on paper. Here are a few recent sketches of mine.

Vighnaharta

Ganapati, the elephant headed god, is also known as Vighnaharta, which literally translates to 'the remover of obstacles'.

Wrath of the protector

Narasimha, in his half-human, half-lion form, casting his divine judgement on the demon Hiranyakashipu. Chennakeshava Temple, Belur, India

Wheel of life

One of the wheels on the Konark sun temple, dedicated to the the source of all life, and the supreme soul who brings light and warmth to the world

The cosmic dance

Shiva, the destroyer of the universe, dancing his cosmic dance evoking ripples of destruction spelling the end of the world. Kedareshwara Temple, Halebidu, India
