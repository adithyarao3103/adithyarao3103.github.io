  Research  
Currently I am working on my masters' thesis, on the topic of Gribov Ambiguity.
The standard procedure for quantizing gauge fields is the Faddeev-Popov quantization, which performs gauge fixing in the path integral formulation and introduces ghost fields. This approach provides the foundation for calculations in quantum Yang-Mills theory. However, Gribov showed that the procedure was incomplete, with residual gauge copies (called Gribov copies) still present in the path integral. These copies impact the infrared behavior of the theory and modify gauge-dependent quantities. Furthermore, their existence breaks down the Faddeev-Popov prescription at a fundamental level.
If you did not understand it, don't worry. Just think that the gauge theory has copies which had to be removed but the standard procedure for removing them doesn't work perfectly.
What am I doing?
I am taking a multifaceted approach towards the problem. On one hand, I am quantitatively studying the problem on the lattice. On the other, I am qualitatively sttudying the stochastic quantization approach which promises a theory without the ambiguity.
